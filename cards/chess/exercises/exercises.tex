\chapter{Exercises}
On the market there are some good chess exercises books. 
I want to make the maximum use of them by writing down my thoughts 
during the exercises. Comparing my thoughts with the answer, I will 
find out my mistakes and improve my thinking skills.

The exercises come from the following books:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Grandmaster Preparation: Calculation} by Jacob Aagaard \cite{aagaard:2012}
    \item \textbf{Endgame Challenge} by John Nunn \cite{nunn:2002}
\end{itemize}
To keep the notes clear, I will use three distinct styles:
\begin{itemize}
  \item My own thoughts: \mythought{italic serif text}. 
  \item My reflection after I see the computer line: \myreflection{teal text}. 
\end{itemize}
